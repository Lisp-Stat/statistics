Online statistics provides single-pass, numerically stable computation of
statistical moments for streaming and large-scale data.  Rather than storing
observations, it accumulates sufficient summary statistics in a
CENTRAL-SAMPLE-MOMENTS object, supporting mean, variance, standard deviation,
skewness, and kurtosis up to the fourth moment.  Partial results from
independent streams can be combined exactly using POOL, enabling
straightforward parallelisation.  Quantile estimation is supported through a
SORTED-REALS accumulator, with both unweighted and weighted variants.
Frequency counting is provided by a SPARSE-COUNTER, which drives TABULATE and
CROSS-TABULATE for categorical data.  All accumulators share a common protocol:
ADD to ingest an observation, TALLY to query the total weight, and POOL to
merge accumulators.  Algorithms follow Bennett et al. (2009) and West (1979).
